Q: We factor a linear map Rm→M through Rn.

  • M is a R-module. Suppose that ϕ:Rm→M and ψ:Rn→M are R-linear maps. Show that if φ is surjective then there is A∈Mn,m(R) such that φ(xA)=ψ(x) for all x∈Rn.

  • Show that if ψ is also surjective then the map Ψ:ker⁡φ→Rm/RnA;y↦y+RnA is surjective and ker⁡Ψ={vA:v∈ker⁡ψ}. Why is Rm/RnA finitely generated?

  • Deduce that if ker⁡ψ is finitely generated then ker⁡ϕ is finitely generated too.

A: These are easy questions if you are allowed to take elements in a given module. In an arbitrary Abelian category (which means linear algebra), one can on longer take elements in any object. However, the statements in your questions still hold.

  1. Hint: since RMod is an Abelian category, (Rm,φ) is the kernel of M⟶00. Hence, for any Rn⟶ψM such that 0∘ψ=0, there exists a unique θ:Rn→Rm such that ψ=φ∘θ.

    The kernel (Rm,φ) is usually simplified into Rm or φ.

    Le diagramme est commutatif for the first part.

  2. Hint: Let (C,π) be cokernel of θ, (K,ι) be kernel of φ. Here Ψ is defined as the composition π∘ι. K↠Ψ~A↪eΨC is the inject-surject decomposition of Ψ. Therefore (K↠Ψ~A,(ι,eΨ)) is the kernel of Rm↠πC in the corresponding morphism category RMod→, M↠bB is the cokernel. The commutative diagram reveals that

    ω∘π∘θ=b∘ψ:Rn→B

    is surjective, but also zero (π∘θ=0 by definition of exact sequences). Thus B=0. The exactness of A↪eΨC↠ωB is induced by the exact sequence K↓A↪Rm↓C↠M↓B, hence eΨ is isomorphism. Consequently, Ψ is surjective.

    Morphism category = viewing morphisms as new objects.

    Le diagramme est commutatif for the second part.

  3. In order to verify the compactness (aka, property of finite generated) of an object in finite dimension, one just need to take and compare the basis.

Q: Show that if R is the ring of continuous functions on ℝ with pointwise addition and multiplication. If R/RA is R-linearly isomorphic to R/RB, then A∼B (A and B differ from the multiplication of unit)?

A: Proposition 1 Let R be any commutative ring. I and J are ideals of R. If R/I≃R/J as R-modules with the standard R-module structure, then I=J. In other words, isomorphic modules have the same annihilators.

Proof of Prop 1 For isomorphic R-modules φ:R/I≃R/J and arbitrary x∈J, we have that

φ(x+I)=xφ(1+I)=x(1+J)=0+J.

Thus x+I∈ker(φ). Since φ is isomorphism, we have that x∈I. It yields that J⊆I. Similarly, I⊆J, thus we have I=J.

Proposition 2 Let R be any commutative ring. I and J are ideals of R. The ring isomorphism φ:R/I≃R/J no longer implies I≃J. Here φ preserves {+,⋅,0,1}. In other words, isomorphic quotient rings have the isomorphic annihilators.

Proof of Prop 2 A counterexample: (1) set R:=C0(R,R), R/(x)≃R/(x−1) by translation. (2) For any commutative ring A, (A×A)/(0×A)≃(A×A)/(A×0).

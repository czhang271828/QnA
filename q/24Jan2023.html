A: Question: Does the additive group A:={x+Q∣x∈R} admit a ring structure with an identity?

Answer: Yes. Since |R|=2ℵ0=2ℵ0−1=|A|, A and R are isomorphic as Q-linear spaces, given by the bijection

φ:R→∼A,rs↦ra(∀r∈Q).

Define the multiplication on A by

a⋅a′=φ(φ−1(a)⋅φ−1(a′)).

Then we can verify

  • (aa′)a″=φ(φ−1(a)φ−1(a′))a″=φ−1(a)φ−1(a′)φ−1(a″), thus (aa′)a″=a(a′a″);

  • φ(1)a=φ(1φ−1(a))=a, thus φ(1)a=a=aφ(1);

  • the commutativity also holds,

  • a(a′+a″)=aa′+aa″, by Q-linearity of φ and φ−1.

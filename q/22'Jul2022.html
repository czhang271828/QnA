Q: The polynomial x5−6x+3∈Q[x] is not solvable by radicals.

A: Indeed, for each irreducible prime-degreed f(x)∈Q[x] with exactly 2 complex roots in C, the Galois group is Sp. This Corollary is pretty helpful.

Proof. Let E denotes the splitting field of f on Q, {ri}i=1p be the set of (distinct) roots of f. Then |Gal(E/Q)|=[E:Q].

Since [Q(r1):Q]=p, p∣|Gal(E/Q)|. Via Sylow I thm., ∃σ∈Gal(E/Q) with order p. When Gal(E/Q) is regarded as a subgroup of Sp, σ is a p-rotation, i.e., p=(1j2j3⋯jp). One notices that {ri}i=1p contains exactly 2 (conjugate) complex roots r1 and r2 (WLOG). Then τ:=(12)∈Gal(E/Q)≤Sp.

It is easy to check that τ and σ generates Sp.

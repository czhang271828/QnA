Q: Is each matrix (in Cn×n) a linear combination of unitary matrices?

A: In fact, each complex matrix A∈Cn×n is a linear combination of at most 2 unitary matrices. Consider the SVD decomposition A=UΣV and write Σ as a linear combination of 2 unitary matrices.

E.g. Write (403−5) into linear combination of 2 unitary matrices.

Step I. Find the SVD decomposition (A=UΣV), i.e.,

(403−5)=(−1525−25−15)(2100010)(−12121212).

Step II. Write Σ as a linear combination of 2 unitary matrices, e.g.,

(2100010)=(210+3i20010+i392)+(210−3i20010−i392).

Step III. Recover A=UΣ1V+UΣ2V, i.e.,

A=120(40+3i10+2i3902i390−3i1030+6i10−i390−50−6i10−i390)+120(40−3i10−2i390−2i390+3i1030−6i10+i390−50+6i10+i390).

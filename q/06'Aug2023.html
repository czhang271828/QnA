Q: How to prove the following lemmas?

  • Let G be simple group and H be a subgroup of index n. Then G≤An.

  • Let G be group and H be a subgroup of index n such that H does not contain any nontrivial normal subgroup of G. Then G≤Sn.

A: USE GROUP ACTION.

Proof of Lemma1: Ω:={gH∣g∈G} is an n -element set. Let S(Ω) denotes the symmertry group of Ω, thus |S(Ω)|=|Sn|=n!. Consider the group action f∈Hom(G,S(Ω)):

x:Ω→Ω,aH↦xaH,∀x∈G.

Then the kernel of the such action satisfies ker(f)⊲G, hence it is either G or {e}. Obviously ker(f)={e}. As a result, G≤S(Ω)=Sn. Since (G∩An)⊲G, G⊲An.

Proof of Lemma2: We also consider the group action f∈Hom(G,S(Ω)):

x:Ω→Ω,aH↦xaH,∀x∈G.

ker(f) is defined as {x∈G∣aH=xaH,∀a∈G}. Since

aH=xaH(∀a∈G)⇔aH∋xa(∀a∈G)⇔x∈∩a∈GaHa−1,

we have that ker(f)=∩a∈GaHa−1≤H. We notice that ker(f)⊲G, hence ker(f)={e}. It yields that G≤Sn.
